Below are Detail about the event and its significance at EASTC.

Celebration of Achievement:

Convocation ceremonies celebrate the academic achievements of students. It's a formal recognition of the hard work and dedication students have put into completing their degrees.

Awarding Degrees:

During the convocation, degrees and diplomas are officially conferred to graduating students. This is a symbolic moment where graduates transition from students to alumni. Acknowledgement of Excellence:

The above two photos shows the Guest of honor awarding graduates who did well

Universities often use convocation ceremonies to acknowledge and reward exceptional academic performance, research contributions, and other notable achievements of students and faculty.

Community Building:

Inspirational Messages:

Distinguished speakers, such as university officials, successful alumni, or notable public figures, deliver speeches that provide inspiration, guidance, and encouragement to graduates as they embark on their post-academic careers.

Tradition and Heritage:

Convocation ceremonies are often steeped in tradition, reflecting the university's history, values, and heritage. This helps instill a sense of pride and continuity among graduates.

Symbolic Closure:

For many students, convocation represents the formal closure of their university journey. It marks the end of one chapter and the beginning of another, be it further education, entering the workforce, or other life pursuits.

Motivation for Current Students:

The ceremony also serves as a motivational event for current students, showing them the tangible rewards of their ongoing efforts and encouraging them to strive towards their own graduation.
